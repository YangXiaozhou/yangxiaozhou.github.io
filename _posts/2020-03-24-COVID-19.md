Signals of Containment
---

### 1. Percentage Change
5-day moving average (MA) of the percentage change in confirmed cases reported in selected countries. It is important to see both the percentage change and the trend of the percentage. To easily classify the situation, we can use the following scale[^percentage]:
- $$r > 100\%$$: **Rapidly increasing**. The total number of cases is doubling everyday.
- $$10\% < r < 100\%$$: **Increasing**. 
- $$1\% < r < 10\%$$: **Slowly increasing**.
- $$r < 1\%$$: **Under control**. There is almost no new cases reported.

### 2. US Testing Numbers
As the containment takes effect, we expect to see the number of positive and negative tests stabalize, and the number of tests pending result drops.

### 3. Google Search Interest in US
This figure tells us how many people in the US are searching for keywords such as "hand sanitizer" or "sympotom". I suspect that as the community spread of the virus is being contained, we can expect to see a drop in searches for words like "sympotom" and "influenza", similar to the trends shown in Singapore.
